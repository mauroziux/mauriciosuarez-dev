Miproteina is a specialized e-commerce platform for sports nutrition supplements serving the Central American market — built from scratch to handle the region's specific logistics, payment, and regulatory requirements.

## The Idea

The sports nutrition market in Central America was underserved by generic e-commerce platforms. Local supplement retailers needed a system that understood regional payment methods, could manage multi-warehouse inventory for diverse product lines, and provided nutritional guidance alongside the shopping experience. Miproteina was conceived as a purpose-built platform rather than a generic storefront — one that combined product catalog, customer education, and operational logistics into a single system.

## The Execution

Built as a Laravel-based e-commerce platform tailored to the regional market:

- **Product catalog and inventory** — structured product data with nutritional information, supplement categories, and real-time stock management across multiple warehouse locations
- **Regional payment integration** — support for local payment methods and currencies specific to the Central American market, alongside standard card processing
- **Order management and logistics** — end-to-end order tracking with integration to regional shipping carriers, handling cross-border deliveries and customs requirements
- **Customer accounts and reordering** — customer profiles with purchase history, supplement regimen tracking, and one-click reordering for recurring purchases
- **Content and education** — integrated nutritional guides and product education content to support informed purchasing decisions

## The Impact

Miproteina serves as a regional e-commerce operation with a purpose-built platform handling the full purchase lifecycle — from product discovery through fulfillment. The platform demonstrates how building for a specific market's requirements (regional payments, cross-border logistics, product-specific inventory) produces a more effective system than adapting generic e-commerce solutions.

The project represents the enterprise platform pattern: identifying the gap between what generic tools provide and what a specific operational context requires, then building the missing pieces as a cohesive system rather than a collection of integrations.
